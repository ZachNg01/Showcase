The Cost of Living Crisis in Australia

Figure 1: Worldwide Cost of Living Index (2022)

According to figure 1, Australia has one of the highest cost of living indexes relative to the rest of the world.

Figure 2: Highest Cost of Living Index (2022)

Australia is one of the country's with the highest cost of living, ranking at 15th place in terms of Cost of Living Index.

Figure 3: Average Price of Food in Australia (2018-2022)

In Australia, the average price of food essentials such as bread, milk, potatoes and eggs have recently exploded in price. Based on figure 3, Eggs alone have doubled in price since 2018.

Figure 4: Living Cost Index VS Consumer Price Index % Change

In Figure 4, It shows the Living Cost Index VS Consumer Price Index % Change.

If the LCI is less than the PCI, the value of the dollar has decreased.

Resulting in the person's income to be of less value.

Figure 5: Cost of Living Index by City in Australia

Figure 5 shows that all Australian cities have a relatively similar high cost of living indexes. Where Sydney is the most expensive city in Australia .

FIgure 6: Distribution of Weekly Income for each Age-Range

Figure 6 shows the distribution of income levels per week for each age group. Those aged 45-54, are more likely to earn $3000 or more per week. Whereas those aged 20-24 are more likely to earn the least at $500-$899 per week.
